Aussiepom

The Aussiepom is a mixed breed dog — a cross between the Australian Shepherd and Pomeranian dog breeds. Affectionate, fun-loving, and nothing short of adorable, these pups inherited some of the best traits from their parents.

Alaskan Klee Kai

Small, smart, and energetic, the Alaskan Klee Kai is a relatively new breed that looks like a smaller version of the Siberian Husky. Even the name “Klee Kai” comes from an Inuit term meaning “small dog.”

Airedale Terrier

Known as the “King of Terriers,” the Airedale is indeed the largest of all terriers. The dog breed originated in the Aire Valley of Yorkshire and was created to catch otters and rats in the region between the Aire and Wharfe Rivers. An able sporting dog, they became an ideal working dog as well, proving their worth during World War I.

Akita Shepherd

An Akita Shepherd is a mixed breed dog–a cross between the Akita and German Shepherd Dog breeds. Stoic, loyal, and hard-working, these pups inherited some of the best traits from both of their parents.

American Foxhound

Easygoing, sweet, kind, and loyal, the American Foxhound dog breed belongs to a way of life that has continued for more than two centuries, but they have the potential to be a modern-day companion as well.

Appenzeller Sennenhunde

The Appenzeller Sennenhunde originated as an all-around farm dog breed, who stayed busy herding the livestock, guarding the farm, and pulling carts in their native Switzerland. Today’s Appenzellers still have the energy, smarts, and self-confidence that makes for valuable working dogs — but they’re anything but low-maintenance.
